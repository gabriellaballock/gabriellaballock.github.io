Talk Pittsburgh: Junior Achievement Interview

Guest Speaker Interview

After JA 18 Under Eighteen Selection

After becoming a Junior Achievement 18 Under Eighteen honoree, I was invited as a guest speaker on Talk Pittsburgh, a local television program featuring influential community members and emerging leaders.

Interview Topics:

  • The projects and events that shaped my leadership journey.
  • Women of Pittsburgh events and mentoring young women in STEM.
  • Project Irreplaceable breast cancer awareness campaign and personal motivation.
  • FIRST Robotics sponsorship and team leadership experiences.
  • My transition to mechanical engineering and passion for manufacturing.
  • Advice for young people pursuing leadership roles in their communities.

Key Message:

Emphasized the importance of authentic engagement, genuine community service, and using your platform to amplify voices in STEM and beyond.

Reflection: This television appearance was an incredible opportunity to share my story with a wider audience and inspire others to pursue leadership roles. It reinforced the power of media in spreading positive messages about community engagement and STEM education.

SLB Radio: 18 Under Eighteen Morning Broadcast

Radio Interview

Morning Show Feature

I was featured on the SLB Radio morning broadcast as part of their series celebrating the 18 Under Eighteen honorees. The interview focused on my personal journey, leadership experiences, and vision for my career in engineering.

Interview Highlights:

  • My transition from civil to mechanical engineering and what sparked the change.
  • Leadership experiences in SHPE, Women of Pittsburgh events, and FIRST Robotics.
  • The importance of mentorship and community service in shaping who I am.
  • My professional goals and interest in manufacturing and structural engineering.
  • Advice for young students pursuing STEM careers and leadership roles.

Audience Reach:

The broadcast reached thousands of listeners across the Pittsburgh area during morning drive time, amplifying messages about STEM education and youth leadership.

Reflection: Radio interviews are a unique platform for connecting with listeners on a personal level. This experience helped me develop confidence in speaking about my journey authentically and encouraged me to continue advocating for STEM education.

Trib Live: Outstanding Young Citizen Feature

Trib Total Media Article

Top 100 Outstanding Young Citizens

I was featured in a Trib Total Media article as one of the Top 100 Outstanding Young Citizens, celebrating individuals making positive contributions to the Pittsburgh community through service, leadership, and dedication to causes they believe in.

Article Focus Areas:

  • My commitment to STEM education and mentoring young women in engineering.
  • Leadership roles in professional organizations like SHPE.
  • Community service initiatives including Women of Pittsburgh events and Project Irreplaceable.
  • Professional development journey and transition to mechanical engineering.
  • Future goals and vision for contributing to the engineering field.

Recognition Impact:

This feature elevated visibility for youth-led initiatives in STEM education and demonstrated the importance of recognizing young leaders in our community who are actively working toward positive change.

Reflection: Being featured in Trib Live was humbling and validating. It affirmed that my work in community service and STEM education is making a meaningful difference. The article serves as documentation of what's possible when you combine passion with action and commitment to serving others.
