SHPE Leadership

Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers

General Body Member → Events Coordinator → Corporate Relations Chair

I began my SHPE journey as a general body member, driven by a desire to grow personally and professionally while supporting my peers. Over time, I stepped into leadership roles that allowed me to directly impact the success and development of our chapter.

Events Coordinator:

  • Led the planning and execution of the annual SHPE Gala, an end-of-year celebration that welcomed over 150 attendees.
  • Highlighted the accomplishments of our members and coordinated with campus departments, corporate partners, catering services, and promotional outreach.
  • Strengthened skills in logistics, communication, and professional collaboration.

Corporate Relations Chair (Current Role):

  • Building and sustaining partnerships with companies and industry professionals.
  • Creating meaningful opportunities for our members—from networking events to corporate info sessions.
  • Redesigned the chapter's sponsorship packet with a refreshed, polished look and stronger alignment with chapter goals and values.

My Mission: To help our chapter grow and provide members with access to internships, mentorships, and full-time opportunities that will propel them forward in their careers.

Women of Pittsburgh Events

Event Organizer & Mentor

2023 - 2024

The Women of Pittsburgh events provided girls with the chance to dream big, pursue their goals, and learn about possible future career opportunities from inspirational women leaders in our community.

2023 Event:

  • Secured five successful female speakers from the Pittsburgh area.
  • Created an engaging environment where girls could ask questions and network with professionals.
  • Event hosted over 100 girls in attendance.

2024 Event (International Women's Day - March 8th):

  • Strategically scheduled for International Women's Day to create a meaningful celebration of women in engineering and beyond.
  • Reached even more girls with over 180 in attendance.
  • Mentored fellow classmates on event planning, preparing them to lead similar initiatives after my graduation.

Reflection: These events demonstrated my commitment to opening doors for the next generation and creating spaces where young women can see themselves in engineering careers.

FIRST Robotics Team Sponsorship

Sponsorship & Outreach Lead

As part of my FIRST Robotics team, I spearheaded outreach initiatives to secure essential sponsorships in the absence of school funding—a critical responsibility for enabling our team to design and build competitive robots.

  • Professional Presentations: Designed and delivered compelling presentations to executives and technical leads across mechanical, electrical, computer, and management fields.
  • Showcase Innovation: Highlighted our robot's engineering innovations, competition strategy, and team impact to secure investor interest.
  • Strategic Relationship Building: Developed professional relationships with industry partners, demonstrating our team's credibility and vision.
  • Resource Acquisition: Successfully secured critical financial and material resources that elevated our ability to design and build a highly advanced, performance-driven robot.

Reflection: This experience taught me the importance of communication, strategic thinking, and persistence—skills that apply far beyond robotics and are crucial for any engineering leader.

Project Irreplaceable Breast Cancer Awareness

Campaign Organizer & Advocate

For the month of October, I partnered with Project Irreplaceable, a letter writing campaign created by a family whose mother was fighting breast cancer. This initiative helps students express their appreciation to special women in their lives—mothers, grandmothers, and mentors—while raising awareness about the importance of early detection and regular self-exams.

My Contribution:

  • Set up a table during lunch where students could pick up letters or pink ribbons to show support.
  • Actively encouraged student participation by sharing the impact of early detection.
  • Created a meaningful, inclusive community event focused on health awareness and gratitude.

Personal Connection: My nana had breast cancer, and it was thanks to early detection that she was able to realize her condition and get treatment in time. This personal connection motivated my dedication to this campaign—early detection truly saves lives.

Reflection: This event reminded me of the power of community action and how engineering skills, combined with genuine care for others, can drive meaningful change both in our workplaces and in our lives.
