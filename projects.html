Umbrella Stand Safety Design

Engineering Design & Development

The Problem: Approximately 7,379 injuries are caused annually by beach and patio umbrellas blowing away and becoming projectiles.

The Solution: Design and manufacture a safer, more stable umbrella stand that prevents wind-related incidents.

Key Features:

  • Wind Speed Monitoring System: Integrated an anemometer and Arduino to convert wind speed into m/s, providing real-time warnings for when to lower or secure the umbrella.
  • Robust Construction: Engineered a durable stand with optimized weight distribution and stability.
  • Manufacturing: Used AutoCAD for design, plasma cutter for cutting, tack welder and rivets for assembly.

Reflection: This project combined mechanical design, electronics, Arduino programming, and real-world manufacturing—showcasing the full product development lifecycle.

Commercial & Residential Building Design

Civil Engineering & Architecture | RIT Course

Throughout my educational journey, I completed several engineering courses through Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT), including Civil Engineering and Architecture. In this course, I had the opportunity to design both commercial and residential buildings that adhered to all applicable codes and standards based on site conditions.

Technical Calculations & Analysis:

  • Live and dead load calculations
  • Door width specifications based on ingress and egress requirements
  • Occupancy load determinations
  • Handicap accessibility compliance
  • Structural and spatial optimization

Reflection: These projects deepened my understanding of building codes, structural safety, and the importance of designing spaces that are both functional and accessible.

Custom Woodworking & Furniture Design

Hands-On Craftsmanship & Precision Work

As a newcomer to woodworking, I gradually built my skills over time with the support of my teacher and mentor, Mr. Barber. I designed and constructed several pieces that blend traditional woodworking techniques with modern digital design.

Major Pieces:

  • Two Custom Side Tables: Featuring precision joinery and refined finishes.
  • Custom Blanket Chest: A larger functional piece combining structural integrity with decorative elements.
  • Epoxy Resin Inlay Details: Incorporated CNC-routed patterns into the wood, which I filled with epoxy resin for a clean, decorative finish.

Materials & Techniques:

  • Premium Wood Selection: Worked with cherry, curly maple, and maple plywood.
  • Precision Finishing: Planed each piece carefully, sanded until smooth, then stained and clear-coated to enhance the grain.
  • Tools & Equipment: Gained confidence using table saw, band saw, drill press, hand tools, and sanding equipment.

Reflection: This hands-on experience helped me develop a deep appreciation for craftsmanship, attention to detail, and seeing an idea come to life from initial design to final product.
