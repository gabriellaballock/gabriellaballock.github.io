Structural/Civil Site Intern

Jacobs

Summer 2025

During my internship at Jacobs, I worked on multidisciplinary projects spanning structural, mechanical, piping, and airport design.

Key Contributions:

  • Structural Modeling: Designed structural models in STAAD, performing load and wind analyses to optimize member sizing.
  • Revit Design: Modeled steel manufacturing components in Revit for facility expansion doubling equipment capacity from 10 to 20 units.
  • Cross-Functional Collaboration: Captured detailed meeting notes and fostered clear communication within teams.
  • Professional Development: Joined JEN networks and completed E3 professional development courses.

Reflection: This experience sharpened both my engineering skillset and my ability to lead and collaborate in complex, fast-paced environments.

Production & Manufacturing Engineering Intern

Giffin Interior & Fixtures

Summer 2023 & Summer 2024 | Pittsburgh, PA

Over two summers, I worked in the engineering department at Giffin Interior and Fixtures, an architectural woodworking company, taking on multiple leadership roles.

Key Contributions:

  • Principal Engineering Drawing Reviewer: Led review and approval of engineering drawings for accuracy and design compliance.
  • 3D Modeling & Site Documentation: Trained in laser measuring and surveying to capture site points for SketchUp models.
  • Electromechanical Project Lead: Designed and assembled a hidden gun cabinet bookcase with actuated pistons and electrical components.

Reflection: This experience gave me hands-on exposure to the full engineering and manufacturing lifecycle, from design to production.

Job Shadow Experience

National Robotics Engineering Center (NREC)

Professional Development

During my job shadow at NREC, I gained hands-on exposure to robotics, automation, and manufacturing processes.

Key Learnings:

  • System Verification & Validation: Assisted in testing Meerkat, a mechatronic arm for camera calibration.
  • Lean Manufacturing: Learned design and manufacturing practices prioritizing efficiency and precision.
  • Hands-On Manufacturing: Fabricated a component using a water jet cutter.
  • Team Collaboration: Observed advanced robotics teams in fast-paced engineering settings.

Reflection: This experience shaped my growing interest in manufacturing within mechanical engineering.

SHPE National Convention

Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers

November 2024 | Anaheim, California

I had the opportunity to attend the SHPE National Convention bringing together over 15,000 students and professionals. Our ERAU SHPE chapter sponsored 50 members, one of our strongest showings yet.

Key Experiences:

  • Leadership & Career Workshops: Participated in workshops on leadership, technical growth, and career readiness.
  • Industry Networking: Connected with professionals from DPR Construction, General Motors, Mortenson, Walt Disney Imagineering, and more.
  • Competitive Interviews: Received interviews from Bechtel, Jacobs, and Whiting-Turner; interview offer from Burns & McDonnell.
  • Mentorship: Strengthened professional growth through MentorSHPE program.

Reflection: The convention reinforced the value of mentorship, networking, and genuine passion for engineering. I left Anaheim inspired and more confident in my future.
